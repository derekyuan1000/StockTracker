# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Retail investors and self-directed traders tracking personal stock and ETF portfolios. They check in at home or on lunch breaks — focused, not rushed. They are financially literate but not professional traders; they want precision and legibility over noise or flash. A subset shares performance publicly via the community leaderboard.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio tracker: log transactions, monitor holdings performance, surface fundamentals, and connect with a community leaderboard. Success means a user can open the app, understand their portfolio health at a glance, and trust the numbers they see.

## Brand Personality

Precise, minimal, financially literate. "Financial data, not financial noise." Serious without being cold; sparse without being empty. Every pixel earns its place.

## Anti-references

- Robinhood: gamification, confetti, neon-green profit porn
- Webull: UI overload, too many panels
- Crypto exchanges: flashy, high saturation, dark-mode neon
- Bloomberg Terminal: dense but inaccessible to non-professionals

## Design Principles

1. **Data legibility first.** Numbers are the product. Contrast, hierarchy, and spacing serve the data — not the other way around.
2. **Ink over decoration.** If an element is not data, a label, or a structural rule, it probably should not exist.
3. **Earned simplicity.** Dense where density informs; spacious where space focuses. Neither extreme by default.
4. **No color without meaning.** Color signals state (up/down, active/inactive). It does not decorate.
5. **Precision signals trust.** Consistent component vocabulary, correct data formatting, no surprising affordances. The tool disappears into the task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Data tables and ticker values must meet 4.5:1 at small mono sizes. Reduced motion respected (already wired). No color-only communication for up/down states (already paired with +/- signs).
